# NeuroNav - Business Model Canvas (Detailed)

## Company: The Neuronavs
### Product: NeuroNav - Adaptive Daily Living Assistant

---

## 1. KEY PARTNERS

**Who are our key partners and suppliers?**

- **Hospitals and rehabilitation centers** that work with patients who have cognitive disabilities
- **Disability organizations and nonprofits** (e.g., foundations, support groups) that connect us with users
- **Apple** as a technology partner (iOS, watchOS, WidgetKit ecosystem)
- **Supabase** as our backend infrastructure provider (database, authentication, real-time)
- **Universities and research centers** for clinical validation and user testing
- **Occupational therapists and caregivers** who recommend the app to their patients
- **Government health agencies** that fund assistive technology programs

---

## 2. KEY ACTIVITIES

**What key activities does our value proposition require?**

- **App development and maintenance** across iOS and watchOS platforms
- **Adaptive UX research** to create interfaces that adjust to each user's cognitive level
- **User testing with real patients** to validate accessibility and usability
- **Caregiver onboarding and training** so they can set up routines for patients
- **Data analysis** on routine completion rates to improve the adaptive engine
- **Partnerships and outreach** with healthcare organizations and disability groups
- **Continuous improvement** of the 5-level adaptive complexity engine
- **Content creation** for pre-built routine templates (cooking, hygiene, medication, etc.)

---

## 3. KEY RESOURCES

**What key resources does our value proposition require?**

- **Development team** with expertise in Swift, SwiftUI, and accessible design
- **Supabase cloud infrastructure** for database, authentication, and real-time sync
- **Apple Developer Program** membership for App Store distribution
- **Adaptive Engine algorithm** (proprietary heuristic engine with 5 complexity levels)
- **Pre-built routine library** with step-by-step guides for daily activities
- **User research data** from patients and caregivers
- **Apple Watch hardware** for haptic feedback, heart rate monitoring, and wrist notifications
- **Clinical advisor network** for medical and therapeutic guidance

---

## 4. VALUE PROPOSITION

**What value do we deliver to the customer?**

- **Adaptive daily living assistant** that adjusts task complexity based on user performance
- **Step-by-step guided routines** for cooking, hygiene, medication, laundry, transit, and more
- **Real-time progress tracking** with stall detection and automatic help prompts
- **Caregiver-patient linking** so caregivers can create and monitor routines remotely
- **Multi-sensory feedback** using voice (TTS), haptics (Apple Watch), and visual cues
- **Safety features** including geofencing (safety zones) and emergency contacts
- **Medication and appointment management** with reminders and tracking
- **Privacy-first design** with Row-Level Security ensuring data protection
- **Accessible interface** designed for adults with cognitive disabilities (simple, clear, supportive)

---

## 5. CUSTOMER RELATIONSHIPS

**What type of relationship does each customer segment expect?**

- **Patient-caregiver bond**: Caregivers set up routines and monitor progress; patients receive guidance
- **Family linking**: Family members can view progress without modifying data
- **Personalized support**: The adaptive engine learns from each user's performance
- **Community building**: Connecting families and caregivers through shared experiences
- **Trust and safety**: All data is private and secure; users feel safe using the app
- **Automated assistance**: Push notifications, TTS voice guidance, and Watch haptics provide ongoing support without human intervention
- **Feedback loops**: Users and caregivers can report issues and suggest improvements

---

## 6. CHANNELS

**Through which channels do our customer segments want to be reached?**

- **Apple App Store** for iOS and watchOS app distribution
- **Google Play Store** for future Android app distribution
- **Hospitals and clinics** as referral points for patients
- **Disability organizations** that recommend assistive technology tools
- **Social media** (Instagram, Facebook) targeting caregivers and families
- **Healthcare conferences and expos** for visibility and networking
- **Word of mouth** from satisfied caregivers and families
- **Web dashboard** for caregivers to manage routines from a computer

---

## 7. CUSTOMER SEGMENTS

**For whom are we creating value?**

- **Adults with cognitive disabilities** (primary users / patients)
  - People with acquired brain injuries
  - People with intellectual disabilities
  - People with neurodegenerative conditions
- **Caregivers** (professional and family caregivers)
  - Occupational therapists
  - Home care aides
  - Support workers in group homes
- **Family members** who want to monitor their loved one's progress
- **Healthcare institutions** (hospitals, clinics, rehabilitation centers)
- **Disability organizations and nonprofits**

---

## 8. COST STRUCTURE

**What are the most important costs in our business model?**

- **Development costs**: Salaries for iOS and backend developers
- **Cloud infrastructure**: Supabase hosting, database, and authentication services
- **Apple Developer Program**: $99/year for App Store distribution
- **Google Play Developer**: $25 one-time fee for Play Store distribution
- **User research and testing**: Costs for clinical trials and user studies
- **Marketing and outreach**: Social media campaigns, conference attendance
- **Design and accessibility audits**: Ensuring the app meets WCAG and accessibility standards
- **Legal and compliance**: Data protection regulations (GDPR, HIPAA considerations)
- **Customer support**: Helping caregivers and families with setup and troubleshooting

---

## 9. REVENUE STREAMS

**For what value are our customers willing to pay?**

- **Freemium model**: Basic features free, premium features require subscription
- **Monthly/Annual subscriptions**: Tiered pricing based on features and number of patients
  - **Basic (Free)**: 1 patient, 3 routines, basic tracking
  - **Standard ($4.99/month)**: Unlimited routines, caregiver linking, safety zones
  - **Professional ($14.99/month)**: Multi-patient management, analytics dashboard, priority support
- **Institutional licensing**: Bulk pricing for hospitals and care facilities
- **Government grants and funding**: Applying for assistive technology grants
- **Partnerships with insurance companies**: Coverage for assistive technology tools
- **Data insights (anonymized)**: Aggregated usage data for healthcare research (with consent)

4. **Apple Watch integration** - Wrist-based guidance with haptic prompts and health monitoring
